Hexapod Dancing Robot

A six-legged, human-rideable robot built for dance performance

CMU Mechatronic Design (18-578 / 16-778 / 24-778) | Spring 2026 | Team 6

Problem Description

Imagine two performers seated atop a giant, six-legged robot, dancing in sync with the machine beneath them. Our Hexapod Dancing Robot is a rectangular-body hexapod featuring 6 legs with 3 degrees of freedom each, designed to carry two riders while performing choreographed walking and dancing movements.

The hexapod allows two people to sit and perform on both sides, with an emergency stop positioned dead center for quick access by both riders. The robot features an IMU-based flip detection system that automatically shuts down the robot if it tips beyond a safe angle, ensuring safety in a live performance environment.

Controlled via a custom wireless controller with joysticks and a speed dial communicating over Bluetooth to an onboard Raspberry Pi, the hexapod is designed for modularity (ships in a 3 ft x 3 ft case), robust construction (supports a 170 lb rider at 2-3 ft off the ground), and expressive motion (tripod gait, wave gait, and custom dance gaits).

Key Features

18 BLDC Motors

ODrive-controlled brushless DC motors — 6 high-torque coxa motors and 12 lighter femur/tibia motors — powering 3 DOF per leg.

Dual-Rider Platform

Supports two performers seated cross-legged or in standard chair position, 2-3 ft off the ground.

Custom Controller

Wireless Bluetooth controller with dual joysticks, potentiometer speed dial, and mode selection buttons.

Safety First

Emergency stop accessible to both riders, IMU-based auto-shutoff on flip, smooth edges throughout.
